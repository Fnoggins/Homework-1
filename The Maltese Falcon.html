The Maltese Falcon

Released October 1941, The Maltese Falcon is a classic film noir tale starring Humphrey Bogart in the lead role as hardboiled detective Sam Spade, and Mary Astor as femme fatale Ruth Wonderly/Brigid O'Shaughnessy. This film is notable for being the first Hollywood pairing of Peter Lorre and Sydney Greenstreet, who went on to be in a number of films together, including Casablanca. In fact, this was Greenstreet's very first film; he had previously been a stage actor, appearing in many productions in London.
