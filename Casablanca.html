Casablanca

Initially released in November 1942, Casablanca starred Humphrey Bogart in a jerk-with-a-heart-of-gold role of the same type he became renowned for portraying, this time as the inimitable Rick Blaine. It also featured the beautiful Ingrid Bergman as Rick's love interest Ilsa Lund, and Paul Henreid as Ilsa's husband Victor Laszlo, a resistance hero previously thought to be dead. The love triangle between the three culminated in a now-famous scene, as depicted above.
